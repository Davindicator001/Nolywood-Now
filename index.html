Nollywood's Global Influence Continues to Grow

Nollywood, Nigeriaâ€™s thriving mainstream film industry, is known for its prolific output, rivaling Hollywood and Bollywood. Originating in the early 1990s with the accidental success of "Living in Bondage", Nollywood films typically feature simple moralistic stories with local cultural themes on modest budgets. The industry's origins extend back to the British colonial era, but economic downturns in the 1980s shifted its focus to video formats due to prohibitive celluloid costs. Nollywood has since evolved, embracing higher production values and achieving a global cultural presence. Despite ongoing challenges with funding and distribution, the appeal of Nollywood persists, offering relatable content and representation. Key films that highlight its evolution include "Glamour Girls", "The Wedding Party", "Lionheart", "Eyimofe", "All the Colours of the World Are Between Black and White", and "Gangs of Lagos". These films reflect various stages of Nollywood's growth and its impact on both local and international audiences.
